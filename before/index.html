Amazon Rainforest

Largest Forest in the World – Amazon Rainforest. This forest has an area of 5.5 Million square kilometres. It includes nine countries in South America. It houses vast and rich trees which are 390 Billion in number. It is region with 16000 species which include variety of plants and animals. It has unique traditions and cultures and is home to indigenous tribes. It contains several rivers including amazon river which is the largest river in the world. It has many unique species like jaguars, anacondas, and harpy eagles.

Congo Rainforest

Second Largest Forest in the World – Congo Forests. Congo Rainforest is the second largest forest in the world. Here are some important features and characteristics of this forest: This rainforest is the second largest tropical rainforest globally following the Amazon. This forest covers an extensive area of approximately 3.4 Million square kilometres in Central Africa. This forest is situated in six countries. There are around 10000 different plant species that live within the borders of this forest. This forest provides a safe place for the very endangered mountain gorilla to live. This rainforest is often referred to as the Lungs of the Planet.

Valdivian Temperate Rainforest

Third Largest Forest in the World – Valdivian Temperate Rainforest Below are a few of the important features of The Valdivian Temperate Rainforest, which is the third-largest forest in the world: This rainforest is found in Chile and Argentina. This forest has different types of trees and it covers a huge area of 248100 in sq km. This forest is one of the most biodiverse ecosystems on earth displaying rich variety of plant and animal life. This rainforest is described by its huge vegetation, tall trees, and rich greenery. Because of its cold and wet weather conditions, this forest has a lot of special plants and animal species. This forest is home to endangered animals like pudu deer as well as South American tapir. It has beautiful landscapes like snow-covered mountains, rivers, and lakes which attract tourists in large numbers. This forest benefits the environment around it by absorbing carbon dioxide and controlling the flow of water (also called water shedding).

Taiga/ Boreal Forest

Fourth Largest Forest in the World – The Taiga or Boreal Forest Following are the features and characteristics of the Boreal forest: This forest is the fourth biggest forest on Earth, and it covers an area of 17 Million square kilometres. It is located in the provinces of Canada and Russia. This forest is made mostly of trees that do not lose their leaves, like spruce, pine, and fir. The Taiga started growing about 12,000 years ago when it got warmer and replaced frozen land. In winter, this forest becomes a cold place that is very chilled and tough to live in. This forest has different types of animals, like bears, wolves, moose, and lynx.

Tongass National Forest

Fifth Largest Forest in the World – Tongass National Forest The Tongass National Forest has the following key features and characteristics as follows: This forest is situated in Southeast Alaska and covers an area of 68000 square kilometres. About 40% of the Tongass is covered by ice, water, muskeg, and rock. This forest is home to the Tlingit, Haida, and Tshimsian People. This forest hosts around 400 species of wildlife. The kinds of trees that are found in this forest are mostly made of red cedar, yellow cedar, and mountain hemlock. This forest is often regarded as a healthy and abundant place. This forest is owned by America and also earns a nickname called “America’s Salmon Forest”.

Great Bear Rainforest

Sixth Largest Forest in the World – Great Bear Rainforest The Great Bear Rainforest has the following key features and characteristics as follows: This forest is located in British Columbia, Canada and it is one of the huge forests of about 64000 square kilometres. This forest is home to some really rare animals like the white Kermode bear, the grizzly bear, and the iconic salmon. This forest includes numerous trees of which four play a vital role which are western red cedar, Douglas fir, western hemlock, and sitka spruce. This forest is also called “Ursa Major” which is a constellation of the northern sky. In this forest the climate ranges from 17 degrees in summers to -2 degrees in winters. We can explore around 1400 bears in this coastal rainforest.

Black Forest

Seventh Largest Forest in the World – Black Forest The Black Forest has the following key features and characteristics: The Black Forest is a big forest in the southwest of Germany. The Romans named it “Black Forest” because of the dark green conifer trees. This forest is known for its typical farmhouses, Black Forest ham, Black Forest gnomes, lakes, rivers, beautiful valleys, and wildlife. The highest mountain in the Black Forest is “Feldberg Mountain” and is 1493 metres tall. The main trees that are found in this forest are fir and pine. “Black forest Ham” is the most popular and most liked product of this forest.
